ADD YOUR PROBLEM STATEMENT HERE

Core Pain Point:
Rural populations rapidly adopting UPI are highly vulnerable to sophisticated digital phishing and social engineering scams due to a lack of practical digital financial literacy.

Target Audience:
Rural Indians and first-time digital payment users who lack awareness about digital fraud and need safe, experiential learning to build secure digital habits and trust in digital banking.

Current Flaws:
Existing financial literacy solutions are text-heavy, unengaging, and lack real-world experiential learning with immediate emotional consequences, rendering them largely ineffective for rural audiences.

--- Slide 3 ---

EXPLAIN YOUR SOLUTION HERE

Core Concept:
BharatQuest is a gamified, offline-capable behavioral financial literacy simulator. It features a 6-state game loop that places users in realistic scam scenarios—such as deceptive WhatsApp-style "UPI Reward" phishing chats. Through cognitive friction and a voice-first AI guide, users must decide whether to interact with malicious links or report them.

Value Proposition:
BharatQuest provides safe experiential learning with immediate simulated consequences. When users fail, they lose simulated money and trust points accompanied by aggressive haptic feedback and RBI educational warnings. This hands-on approach builds genuine caution and practical scam-defense skills far more effectively than traditional text-based warnings.
